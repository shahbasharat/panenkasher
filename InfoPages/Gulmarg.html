HISTORY

Gulmarg: A Meadow of Timeless Beauty and Stories

Nestled in the lap of the Himalayas, Gulmarg has a history as enchanting as its scenic beauty. Centuries ago, Mughal emperors sought solace in its cool meadows, giving it the name "Meadow of Flowers. "During the British colonial era, Gulmarg became a favored retreat, and the establishment of the Gulmarg Golf Course added to its allure. Post-independence, it evolved into the "Winter Sports Capital of India," with skiing becoming a major attraction. Today, Gulmarg stands not only as a skiing paradise but as a year-round destination, inviting visitors to explore its rich cultural tapestry and immerse themselves in the beauty of the Himalayan landscape.

Gulmarg's history is a journey through time, from ancient Mughal retreats to colonial getaways, and now a modern tourist hotspot. The lush meadows and snow-capped peaks tell stories of emperors and adventurers, creating a timeless narrative. Whether riding the iconic Gulmarg Gondola or exploring the historic St. Mary's Church, every step in Gulmarg unveils a piece of its fascinating past. As visitors embrace the simplicity of the "Meadow of Flowers," they become part of a living history, where nature and culture coexist in harmony, inviting all to share in the stories etched into the heart of Gulmarg.
